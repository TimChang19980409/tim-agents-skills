# Interaction Plan

## When to use

Use when the user wants classroom interaction, workshop activities, learner prompts, exercises, retrieval questions, discussion flow, or feedback checkpoints.

## Inputs

- Audience size, skill level, and delivery environment
- Time budget and facilitation constraints
- Topic and target learner behavior
- Whether learners can write code, discuss, answer polls, or work in groups

## Steps

1. Choose interaction types that match the environment: prediction, retrieval, compare, debug, explain, design, or apply.
2. Place interactions before and after key explanations, not only at the end.
3. Design prompts that learners can attempt with the knowledge already established.
4. Add expected responses, likely mistakes, and facilitator follow-up.
5. Include a final retrieval or transfer activity.

## Safety gates

- Do not ask learners to perform before they have enough scaffolding.
- Do not use interaction as decoration; every activity must support a learning goal.
- Do not rely only on volunteers when the topic is risky or confidence-sensitive.

## Outputs

- A timed interaction plan
- Prompts and activities
- Expected learner responses
- Feedback or debrief notes
- Retrieval and transfer checks

## Verification

- Confirm every interaction has a clear learner action.
- Check that activities vary in difficulty and format.
- Ensure the plan includes a low-pressure way for quiet learners to participate.
